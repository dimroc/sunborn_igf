terrain



The terrain is implemented as tiles inside a standard Quad Tree with view frustum culling. Each tile has a precomputed triangle strip also known as a 'chunk'. Across the whole terrain there are only 3 index buffers used for all tiles, representing the 3 levels of detail.



Gaps between tiles because of different levels of details are filled with 'skirts'.


With the use of vertex shaders, we 'Geomorph' the tiles between the different level of details by interpolating between a lower detail position to a higher detail position. Distant mountains retain a suitable level of detail since we check the 'screen space error' metric.




Texture splatting is featured through the use of shaders and a blend map. This Blend Map uses it's ARGB components to dictate the amount a texture is used to determine a pixel's color.



The load limit of our terrain renderer is determined by the computer's RAM. Please note that these textures are not final.
